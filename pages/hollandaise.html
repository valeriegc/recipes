Hollandaise

Description

The first origin of the famous Hollandaise sauce dates back as far as to the 16th century. The sauce that later on became to known also as one of the so called "Mother sauces" of the French cuisine, was first developed in the French cuisine. However, the roots of the infamous sauce remain unlear, as there has also been a speculation revolving around its origins: the sauce may be in fact a descendant of the Dutch cuisine.

The main ingredients of the Hollandaise sauce are eggs, butter and lemon. The incredients, as well as the entire reciple, can be found below.

The ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon of water
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 0.5 cups of butter
  • 1/4 tablespoon salt
  • to be added later: 0.5 cups butter

Preparation

  1. Add all the incredients to a blender, except for the 0.5 cups of butter that is mentioned in the end of the list.
  2. When the sauce is formed, add the remaining amount of butter.
  3. Enjoy your new hollandaise sauce!
